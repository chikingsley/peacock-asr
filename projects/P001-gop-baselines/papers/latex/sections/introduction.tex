\section{Introduction}

Computer-assisted pronunciation training requires a scoring pipeline that maps
learner speech to phone-level or utterance-level judgments that correlate with
human ratings. In this project, the narrow question is not whether large speech
models can recognize speech in general, but which scorer family best converts
phoneme posterior evidence into pronunciation scores on a fixed benchmark.

The completed P001 campaign isolates that question. We compare multiple
posterior backends under a common scoring contract and evaluate three scorer
families: scalar GOP-based regression, richer feature regression, and a
contextual GOPT scorer. The paper should ultimately argue for a clean boundary:
backbone quality and scorer quality are separable axes, and this benchmark is
primarily about the latter.

The final introduction should be rewritten against the target venue and should
explicitly define CAPT, GOP, segmentation-free scoring, and the role of
SpeechOcean762 in the evaluation protocol.
